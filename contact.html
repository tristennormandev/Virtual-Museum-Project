  • Anti-War Rally
  • This photograph was taken at the height of the Anti-War Movement, and shows a large-scale counterculture rally taking place in Philadelphia, Pa. Furthermore, this rally takes place at the John F. Kennedy Plaza. The location chosen for this demonstration is heavily populated, and this shows key insight into the minds of protesters. Protesters were proud of their stance and wanted to be noticed. Despite the large number of people involved, the demonstration appears exceptionally organized, furthering the theme of unity cultivated by the movement. This rally also consisted of men, women, and children. The diversity of counterculture supporters furthers these ideas of peace and unity.

    Gottlieb, Sonnee. Anti-war rally. 1970-10-31. Retrieved from the Digital Public Library of America. (Link)
  • Local Anti-War Poster
  • This poster was created to advertise a local play centered around the themes of the Anti-War Movement. This poster was also created during the peak of the Anti-War Movement in Denver, CO. The content of this poster consists of a large, bold title. The attention-grabbing nature of the title furthers the idea that members of the counterculture had a strong belief in their stance and had no shame supporting their cause. The poster being created by hand alludes to the event being a smaller-scale, local event. This shows that the Anti-War Movement reached beyond the politically involved to small towns and communities. Finally, the messages of counterculture are being communicated through the format of a play. This shows the creative and artistic nature of the Anti-War Movement that was a driving force in its popularity.

    Flier on Anti-War Play, 1970. 1970. Retrieved from the Digital Public Library of America. (Link)

  • Campus Protest
  • While the exact date this photograph was taken is unknown, it is known that it was taken around the early 1970s. This photograph was taken on a university campus in San Jose, CA. Already, the location being a university campus shows us not only the local significance of the Anti-War Movement, but it shows us that the counterculture was very important to the youth. This furthers the themes of diversity that surrounded the Anti-War Movement. The man pictured is seen shouting through a megaphone. The use of a megaphone in this image further shows that protestors were proud of what they believed and wanted to be heard.
